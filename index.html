01 / The Eduba frame

Most organizations automate the wrong things.

About 60 percent of what a team does every day is traditional code and database work. About 30 percent is rule-based logic. Around 10 percent is a real AI problem. The work is sorting those three into the right layers before anything is built.

Applied to a small foundation whose operating shape is organizational and context-layered by design, the map resolves quickly. Fellow records, mentor network, partner-company relationships, and donor stewardship are database and code work. Intake eligibility, partner-placement fit, and cohort event scheduling are rule-based logic. Outcome pattern matching across cohorts, matching at quality rather than availability, and content capture from mentorship conversations are where AI actually earns its keep.

Sorting those three into the right layers is the same methodology discipline the foundation already brings to the transition itself.

  2. 30%Rule-based logic
